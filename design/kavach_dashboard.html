How to read this board

KAVACH is an industrial-safety intelligence layer. Conventional plants have working alarms and interlocks, but each watches one sensor alone — so a compound hazard (several small things going wrong together) can stay invisible until it is too late. KAVACH fuses sensors, work permits, shift handovers and plant layout into a single 0-100 risk score, and raises an evidence-backed alert hours before any single alarm would react.

This board replays a past coke-oven gas incident. You are viewing 06:30, the moment KAVACH first flagged critical risk. The first conventional single-sensor alarm did not trip until 09:39 — 3h 09m later. All data is synthetic, a composite inspired by publicly reported incidents.
